Product Discovery Support

Tools and processes to help PMs understand what to build next

üéØ Jobs-to-be-Done Interviews

Understand the underlying job, not just the feature request

  • When did you first realize you needed this?
  • What were you doing before?
  • What does success look like?

üìä Usage Analytics

Let behavior tell you what words can't

  • Power users: what do they do differently?
  • Drop-off points: where do people get stuck?
  • Feature adoption: used vs ignored?

üèÜ Win/Loss Analysis

Interview customers who chose you AND those who didn't

  • What tipped the decision?
  • What did competitors offer?
  • What almost made you choose differently?

üî¨ Design Partner Programs

Co-develop with customers who have the problem

  • 5-8 partners, not too many
  • Weekly check-ins, not monthly
  • Ship to them first, iterate fast

üåê Community Listening

Monitor where Mistral users talk

  • r/LocalLLaMA, r/MachineLearning
  • HuggingFace discussions
  • X/Twitter, LinkedIn, Discord

üî¨ Hypothesis Testing

Verify before building

  • 5 requests = hypothesis, not feature
  • Talk to 3x the sample to verify
  • Look for unprompted mentions

Driving Adoption

How to implement across teams and ensure buy-in

üöÄ Implementation Playbook

1

Start with Pain, Not Process

Don't pitch "a new feedback system." Lead with: "We lost a ‚Ç¨500k deal because we didn't know 3 other customers wanted the same feature."

2

Pilot with One Strategic Account

Pick your biggest customer. Set up the full loop just for them. Prove it works before scaling.

3

Make Capture Effortless

If it takes >30 seconds to log feedback, nobody will do it. Use Slack emoji reactions, auto-capture from calls.

4

Weekly "Voice of Customer" Ritual

15-min weekly meeting where PM shares top themes. Invite Sales/CS. Make it a habit.

5

Close the Loop Visibly

When you ship something a customer asked for, make a big deal of it. This is the incentive for everyone to participate.

6

Measure and Share Impact

Track: time from feedback to ship, NPS changes, expansion revenue. Share wins monthly.

‚úÖ What Works

  • Executive sponsor who champions it
  • Effortless capture (< 30 seconds)
  • Visible wins ("customer X got feature Y")
  • Regular rituals (weekly review)
  • Clear ownership (Product Ops)

‚ùå What Fails

  • Over-engineering before proving value
  • No ownership = system decays
  • All capture, no action = people stop contributing
  • No exec sponsor = Sales won't prioritize
  • Too much process, not enough value
